Future World

Future World consists of a variety of pavilions that explore innovative aspects and applications including technology and science. Future World also serves as the park's main entrance and features the park's iconic landmark, Spaceship Earth, a large geodesic sphere structure which houses a themed attraction inside. Originally, each pavilion of Future World featured a unique circular logo which was featured on park signage and the attractions themselves. The logos, including that of Epcot itself, have been phased out over recent years, but some remnants still remain scattered throughout the park, the pavilions are now instead identified by name and recognized by the main attraction(s) housed inside.

Tips:

EPCOT isn't designed like the other parks since its only divided into two sections instead of four or five. This means that all the major attractions (Soarin', Mission:Space, and Test Track) are within Future World. Soarin' is EPCOT's most popular attraction and probably the most FastPass necessary attraction on Disney property. You'll also need a FastPass for Test Track, especially since it was renovated in 2012. Mission: Space doesn't pull in nearly as long wait times as Test Track or Soarin' so a FastPass isn't necessary.
