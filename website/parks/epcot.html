EPCOT

Epcot is the second of four theme parks built at Walt Disney World in Bay Lake, Florida, near Orlando, Florida. It opened as EPCOT Center on October 1, 1982, and spans 300 acres, more than twice the size of theMagic Kingdom park. It is dedicated to the celebration of human achievement, namely technological innovation and international culture, and is often referred to as a "Permanent World's Fair." In 2013, the park hosted approximately 11.22 million guests, making it the fifth most visited theme park in the world. The park is represented by Spaceship Earth, a geodesic sphere that also serves as an attraction.

Walt Disney's original idea for Epcot was that of a real community or small town that would be centered around modern technological developments. The name "Epcot" is actually an acronym for Experimental Prototype Community Of Tomorrow.

Epcot is divided into two themed areas:

  1. Future World
  2. World Showcase

As it's name suggests, Future World is focused more on technology and the developments of the future. Here, guests will find the more thrilling attractions Epcot has to offer.

World Showcase is focused on international culture, featuring 12 different countries to visit. World Showcase provides a different sort of experience than Future World. Here, guests won't find many thrill rides, but instead guests have the opportunity to take a small step into what the international community has to offer. Guests can sample different food and drinks from around the world, as well as listen to some native music from the host countries. World Showcase offers a chance to take a more leisurely approach to your Disney Vacation.

As the day comes to an end, World Showcase Lagoon is lit-up by the surrounding torches, setting the tone for the fireworks and pyrotechnic spectacular Illuminations: Reflections of Earth.
